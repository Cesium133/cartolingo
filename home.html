What is CartoLingo?

CartoLingo is a resource for citizens and researchers with a goal to understand more about the languages that are present in their community and around the world. In an increasingly multicultural society, we interact with many who speak different languages. With CartoLingo, you can observe which languages are spoken in communities.

Don't see any listed languages for your community? It's easy to submit data!


How does data get submitted to CartoLingo?

Users like you with a desire to see the languages of their communities recognized submit data on our Submit page. The language listed for each polygon is what the submitting user understands to be the primarily spoken language in the area.


What uses could CartoLingo serve?

The data posted on CartoLingo serves many different user-bases. First is the casual user who is curious about languages spoken anywhere around the world. The second type of user, and the one CartoLingo aims to help the most, are Emergency Response Personnel who wish to understand a region before they are deployed to provide assistance for it. It's imperative to have the right tools to provide aid to those who don't speak the official language(s) of the region, and this means employing or enlisting those who do speak the lesser-spoken languages.

CartoLingo aims to be the ultimate source for Federal agencies and emergency operations that serve disaster-stricken communities at a time when they need help the most.


How do I use CartoLingo?

To submit data to the CartoLingo database, just head on over to the Submit page and start submitting data. It's really easy!
